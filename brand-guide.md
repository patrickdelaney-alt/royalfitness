# Royal — Brand Guide

---

## Visual identity in one sentence

Royal looks like a painting of a place you'd rather be: still, saturated, architectural, expensive.

---

## Colour palette

### Primary colours

| Name         | Hex       | Use                                      |
|--------------|-----------|------------------------------------------|
| Cream/linen  | #f5f2ec   | All backgrounds. The base of everything. |
| Forest green | #2d5a27   | Primary text, buttons, key UI elements   |
| Gold         | #c8a951   | Accents, highlights, premium details     |

### Extended palette (reference)

| Name               | Hex       | Use                                                        |
|--------------------|-----------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| Cobalt sky         | #1a2e6e   | Deep blue from the paintings — use sparingly for impact    |
| Architectural white| #f8f7f4   | Cards, surfaces, clean UI panels                           |
| Lush green         | #1e4d2b   | Darker green for depth, hover states                       |
| Warm shadow        | #c4a882   | Subtle dividers, muted text, secondary elements            |

### Colour rules

- The cream background (#f5f2ec) is non-negotiable. It is the single most important brand element.
- Never use pure white (#ffffff) or pure black (#000000) in the UI.
- Gold is an accent, not a primary colour — use it at roughly 10% of the visual weight of green.
- The cobalt blue (#1a2e6e) references the deep sky in the paintings. Reserve it for moments that need gravity — a hero section, a splash screen, a key campaign image.
- Avoid gradients. The paintings use flat, saturated colour fields. So do we.

## Typography

### How it works in the app

- **Wordmark/logo only**: Cormorant Garamond (serif) — the single editorial anchor of the brand. It appears once, at the top of every screen, and that's enough.
- **All UI text**: Plus Jakarta Sans (sans-serif), light-to-regular weight, generous sizing. Filter pills, card text, post content, labels — all sans-serif.

### The rule

Do not introduce serif into body UI. Its power comes entirely from restraint. One deliberate typographic statement at the top of the screen, then quiet. This is the "Earned Silence" principle applied to type.

### General type guidelines

- **Weight**: Light to regular always. Never heavy or bold except for single-word emphasis in headers.
- **Size**: Err large. Generous type with generous spacing communicates confidence.
- **Casing**: Sentence case everywhere. Never ALL CAPS. Never title case on body text.
- **Line height**: Generous — never cramped. Copy should breathe.

## Design philosophy — "Earned Silence"

The paintings that inspired Royal share a quality: they are completely still. No clutter, no noise, no urgency. Everything that is in the frame is meant to be there.

Apply this to every design decision:

- **70% negative space** — if a screen feels full, remove something
- **One focal point per view** — the eye should know immediately where to go
- **Nothing decorative** — every element must earn its presence
- **No urgency** — no badges screaming for attention, no notification dots on everything, no red unless it genuinely means an error

The question to ask about any design element: does this need to be here, or is it just filling space?

## Mood and reference images

Two paintings anchor the aesthetic:

**Painting 1** — Pool with palm tree and lush garden. Deep cobalt sky, saturated greens, graphic flat quality. Stillness. The pool is empty but the scene feels complete.

**Painting 2** — Mid-century modern house, pool, manicured lawn. Same cobalt sky. Architectural white against vivid green. Lounge chairs, no people. Aspirational but not aspirational in a trying-hard way.

What these images communicate:
- Wealth that doesn't announce itself
- Summer, California, the good life — but quiet about it
- Architecture over decoration
- The feeling of having arrived somewhere worth being

When generating any visual asset, hold these images in mind. If the output could belong in either painting, it's on brand.

---

## What Royal is not

- Not a gym app (no neon, no aggressive energy, no "CRUSH IT" copy)
- Not a tech startup (no gradients, no glassmorphism, no dark mode as default)
- Not mass market (no generic stock photography, no crowded UIs, no friendly rounded everything)
- Not trying hard (no confetti, no gamification badges, no animated everything)

---

## Asset checklist

Before publishing any visual asset, check:

- [ ] Background is cream (#f5f2ec) or cobalt (#1a2e6e) — not white, not grey
- [ ] Negative space is at least 50% of the frame
- [ ] No more than 2 colours used prominently in a single asset
- [ ] Typography is Cormorant Garamond (wordmark) or Plus Jakarta Sans (UI) — never display or novelty fonts
- [ ] No gradients, no drop shadows, no glass effects
- [ ] If there are people, they are at rest — not mid-workout, not grimacing
- [ ] The overall feeling is: calm, earned, somewhere worth being
