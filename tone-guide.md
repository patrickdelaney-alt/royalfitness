# Royal — Tone Guide

---

## The voice in one sentence

Royal sounds like a person who takes their health seriously, doesn't perform it for anyone, and happens to get paid for the things they already recommend.

---

## Who we are writing for

Royal is not just for wellness influencers. It is for anyone who genuinely cares about how they live — the person who has a great gym they always tell friends about, a protein powder they actually use, a recovery routine they've quietly dialled in. They are not building a brand. They are just living well, and Royal lets them earn from that quietly.

The royalties feature should feel like a natural extension of this — not a sales pitch, not an income opportunity, but simply: if people follow what you do, you earn a little from it.

---

## The royalties concept — how to talk about it

This is the most important and most delicate part of the brand voice.

**What it is mechanically**: A referral commission. If someone uses your link to buy a product, you earn a percentage.

**What it should feel like**: Earning royalties on your lifestyle. The same way a musician earns royalties when their work gets used — passive, tasteful, a recognition of influence rather than a transaction.

**The framing to use**:
- "Earn royalties" — yes
- "Your referral link" — acceptable, understated
- "Get paid for your wellness" — yes, if kept brief
- "Earn from what you already do" — yes

**The framing to avoid**:
- "Affiliate links" — too transactional, sounds like a blog circa 2014
- "Commission" — too salesy
- "Make money" — too blunt, wrong register for the brand
- "Monetise your content" — sounds like a YouTube tutorial
- "Passive income" — sounds like a get-rich-quick scheme
- "Cash in on your followers" — never

**The underlying principle**: The royalties idea only works if the rest of the app is genuine. Royal is first a place where people log real workouts, share real meals, track real wellness. The earning layer sits on top of that authentically — it does not lead. Copy should reflect this order of priority.

---

## Tone attributes

**Understated** — say less than you could. A caption that ends one beat early is better than one that explains itself.

**Confident, not loud** — Royal doesn't need to convince anyone. The writing assumes the reader already gets it.

**Warm but not friendly** — there is a difference. Friendly is exclamation marks and emoji. Warm is genuine, human, not corporate — but still has some reserve.

**Real, not aspirational** — we are not selling a lifestyle fantasy. We are talking to someone already living one. "Little nighttime squash" is perfect Royal energy. "Transform your body in 30 days" is not.

**No hustle** — nothing about grinding, crushing it, pushing limits. Effort is assumed, not performed.

## Examples

### Captions that sound like Royal

- "Quiet morning. Matcha, stretch, done."
- "Been recommending this to everyone for two years. Now you can actually earn when you do the same."
- "Recovery is training. Treated it that way today."
- "Added a referral link to the sauna mat I've used every week for a year. Feels right."
- "Not every session needs to be documented. This one did."

### Captions that do not sound like Royal

- "Use my link to save 20%!! 🔥🔥"
- "Guys I am OBSESSED with this product"
- "Ad | Gifted | Paid partnership" (the disclosure is fine and legally required — the energy around it shouldn't be)
- "Swipe up to shop my entire wellness routine!"
- "This changed my life — no joke"

---

## Referral link copy — specific guidance

When writing UI copy, notifications, or marketing around the royalties/referral feature, the tone should feel like an invitation, not a sales funnel.

Good:
- "Add a referral link to this post"
- "Earn royalties when people use your recommendations"
- "Your wellness, your earnings"
- "Share what you actually use"

Avoid:
- "Monetise this post"
- "Add your affiliate link"
- "Start earning today!"
- "Turn your followers into income"

---

## The one-line test

Before publishing any copy, read it aloud and ask: could this have been said by someone who genuinely doesn't need the money but shares it anyway because it's good?

If yes — it's Royal.
If it sounds like someone trying to make rent through Instagram — rewrite it.
